## Format

### Round 1 — Individual Net Stableford

- Each player plays their own ball throughout the round.
- Handicaps are applied to determine net scores on each hole.
- Points are awarded per hole as follows:

| Result | Points |
|---|---|
| Eagle or better | 4 |
| Birdie | 3 |
| Par | 2 |
| Bogey | 1 |
| Double bogey or worse | 0 |

- The player with the **most total Stableford points** wins Round 1.
- Maximum score per hole: **Net Double Bogey** (scores above this are capped at 0 points).

---

### Round 2 — 2-Man Best Ball (Net)

- Players are divided into **4 teams of 2**.
- *(Team pairings to be determined prior to the round — e.g., random draw or commissioner's discretion.)*
- Both players play their own ball on every hole.
- Handicaps are applied to each player's score.
- The **lower net score** between the two partners counts as the team score for each hole.
- Final team score = sum of best net scores across all 18 holes.
- The team with the **lowest total best ball net score** wins Round 2.
- Maximum score per hole per player: **Net Double Bogey**.

---

### Round 3 — Individual Net Stroke Play

- Each player plays their own ball throughout the round.
- Handicaps are applied to produce a final net score.
- The player with the **lowest net total** wins Round 3.
- Maximum score per hole: **Net Double Bogey**.
- **Final round tee times:** Leaders go out last (reverse standings order).

## General Rules

- Play is governed by the **Rules of Golf** as published by the USGA/R&A, with the local rules of the host course in effect.
- **Maximum score per hole:** Net Double Bogey. Pick up once this is reached to maintain pace of play.
- **Pace of play** is expected throughout — ready golf is encouraged.
- All disputes are settled by a majority ruling of the group, with the commissioner having final say.

---

## Tiebreakers

### Round Tiebreakers
- If two or more players tie for a round win, the round payout is split equally unless all players agree to a playoff before the result is official.

### Overall Standings Tiebreakers
1. Head-to-head points across all rounds between the tied players.
2. Points in Round 3 (most recent round).
3. Points in Round 1.
4. If still tied, the payout is split equally.
